Natural disaster patterns in Australia reveal that environmental disasters and extreme weather together comprise nearly half of total disasters, dominating the landscape of natural hazards. Fire-related incidents form another significant portion, highlighting Australia's ongoing vulnerability to bushfires. Additionally, shipwrecks caused by natural disasters, such as severe storms and cyclones, have historically contributed to the toll on Australia's coastal regions. This distribution of natural disasters reflects Australia's unique geographic and climatic challenges, emphasising the critical need for robust disaster management strategies across land and sea.
